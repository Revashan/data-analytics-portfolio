## Project Overview
Customer churn is a critical business challenge for subscription-based companies. 
This project analyzes customer churn for a telecom company to identify key churn drivers, quantify revenue at risk, and propose actionable retention strategies.

The analysis simulates a real-world enterprise churn investigation typically requested by Customer Experience and Revenue teams.

---

## Business Problem
The telecom company observed increasing customer attrition, especially among newly onboarded customers.
Leadership requested answers to:
- Who is churning?
- Why are they churning?
- What actions can reduce churn?

---

## Objectives
- Identify high-risk customer segments
- Analyze churn drivers (contract type, tenure, payment behavior)
- Estimate revenue at risk due to churn
- Recommend data-backed retention strategies

---

## Dataset
- Telco Customer Churn Dataset (IBM Sample)
- ~7,000 customer records
- Includes customer demographics, tenure, billing, contract type, and churn flag

---

## Tools & Technologies
- Python (Pandas, Matplotlib)
- SQL
- Power BI
- GitHub (Documentation)

---

## Key Metrics
- Churn Rate (%)
- Revenue at Risk
- Average Tenure of Churned Customers
- High-Risk Customer Segments

---

## Analysis Summary
- Calculated overall churn rate and revenue impact
- Segmented churn by contract type, tenure, and payment method
- Identified early-life churn patterns (first 6 months)

---

## Deliverables
- Python notebook for data cleaning and analysis
- SQL scripts for business queries
- Power BI dashboard with executive insights
- Business recommendations document

---

## Outcome
This project provides a clear churn reduction roadmap aligned with business KPIs and operational decision-making.
